One-page Topic Outline for Research Proposal

Due Tuesday Oct. 7 in class

As a reminder, the final 10 page research proposal should include:

  • a research question
  • why that question is interesting to economists
  • some economic theory that gives a hypothesis to test
  • this should necessitate a literature review
  • identification of a data source (you don't have to get it, but you have to be specific about what it is, what variables it has that you'll use, or how you will construct a data source)
  • identification strategy: how will you cleanly demonstrate (econometrically) the effect you're seeking to find?
  • explain the direction of the expected results this should have already been done if the previous sections are done correctly

The goal of this one-page outline is to make sure that you get started on the right track with an answerable question.

Your outline should include

  • the research question you'd like to tackle

"Does schooling affect earnings?" is not a research question ready to go, "Did xx change in schooling law in Quebec impact the profile of male vs. female earnings" is a question that shows you've put some thought into the process,and then you should be able to say why we expect a differential male vs. female response.

  • If you are proposing to investigate the impact of a change in law, you should tell me about the law.
  • If you are proposing to analyze incentives in government behavior, you should give background on what those incentives are.
  • Some hint that data are availeble for this qustion, or that you're looking into it.
  • And a little bit more about some part of the project - the data, the literature, or the theory.
